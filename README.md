**Blinkit Excel Dashboard – Project Analysis**

**1. About the Project**

Project Name: Blinkit Grocery Sales Dashboard
What it is:
This is an Excel-based Sales Analytics Dashboard for Blinkit — India's Last Minute App (quick grocery delivery service). It analyzes grocery outlet sales performance across different outlet types, sizes, locations (Tier 1, 2, 3 cities), and product categories.
Purpose / Goal:
The main goal is to give a complete picture of:

  * How much total revenue Blinkit outlets are generating
  * Which outlet sizes and locations perform best
  * Which item types sell the most
  * How revenue has changed year by year (2011–2022)
  * What the fat content split (Low Fat vs Regular) looks like across tiers

**2. Key Insights**

Outlet Size Performance

There are 3 outlet sizes: Small, Medium, and High
Medium outlets generate the highest revenue — $508K
High outlets come second — $249K
Small outlets contribute $445K — surprisingly close to Medium, suggesting there are many small outlets
The donut chart clearly shows Medium dominates in sales value

Outlet Location (Tier Performance)

Tier 3 cities are the top performers with $472K in sales (shown in green in the Outlier Location chart)
Tier 2 cities come second with $393K (shown in yellow/gold)
Tier 1 cities generate the least — shown in red (value partially visible, appears to be around $336K)
This is a key insight — smaller cities (Tier 3) are outperforming metro cities (Tier 1) for Blinkit

Item Type Sales

Snack Foods and Frozen Foods are top sellers at $0.18M each
Household items come next at $0.14M
Dairy and Health & Hygiene each contribute around $0.10M and $0.07M
Breakfast, Baking Goods, and Breads are the lowest performers (around $0.02M–$0.04M)
Snack Foods clearly dominating shows customer preference for ready-to-eat items

Fat Content Split

Low Fat products generate $776.3K — the majority of sales
Regular fat products contribute $425.4K
Across all tiers (Tier 1, 2, 3), Low Fat consistently outsells Regular fat items
This suggests Blinkit customers are health-conscious and prefer low-fat options

Yearly Revenue Trend (2011–2022)

Revenue started at $78K in 2011 and grew steadily
Hit $130K–$132K range from 2012 to 2016 (stable period)
Big spike in 2018 — reached $205K (best year)
Dropped slightly to $129K–$133K range in 2019–2020
Recovered to $131K in 2022
The 2018 peak likely reflects aggressive expansion or a high-demand period

Customer Ratings

Average rating of 3.92/5 — reflects generally positive customer satisfaction
Consistent with a growing but still-maturing delivery service
